Hello Al, In utilizing what you use for trading on your 1 minute chart, I thought that before I posted to the thread, I wanted to make sure that you were ok with some suggestions I would like to make. First, I would add four important levels to your Trix indicator, at levels, 0.03, -0.03, 0.1 and -0.1. These will used to determine the strength of a particular move and second, will give you the ability to exit your current trade before you get an arrow going in the opposite direction. Second, I would eliminate the bottom CCI chart and use a 50 and 200 ema average to let you know how the market is in relationship to those averages. Now, for the trading idea(s): I would still use the up or down arrow to get into a trade and then watch the Trix indicator as it moves up to the 0.03 0r -0.03 lines (there is no special reason why these numbers were suggested, but through eyeballing a chart it appears to be a threshold that the indicator should get through to show some strength.) The key to finding out whether there is strength in the market is to make sure that the indicator is strong enough to go above those lines. If it can't, most probably you have two things going on, first, the market is going to go in the opposite direction because of where price is in relation to the two ema's or the market is in a very quiet time and best not trade. So, when that indicator goes above or below that =/- 0.03 level when it reaches it's apex or nadir and it starts backing off in the next bar from its extreme I would exit the trade. In general one will do better this way than waiting for a reverse signal. Now, will there be times when the indicator backs off and price continues in the same direction, the answer is yes, but, if one is looking to grab a piece of the pie and you did, then you did well. Finally when the indicator goes over the +/- 0.10 levels you have an extreme situation where again when the bar pulls back from those levels, I would suggest exiting, even though you might not get the highest or lowest price, you will for sure make some money. So, Al, there you have it, a way to help protect and keep some of those profits that come your way without having to give back too much or have a loss. Let me know what you think or if you have any questions. All the best and great job of setting up this thread idea. WGT
