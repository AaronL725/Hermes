Cube thanks very much for feedback I must respond to my system signal After 1700pips so far this month I feel its my trusted friend (system) Having said this your comment reminded me what Mark Douglas said in "Trading in the Zone" 3. There is a random distribution between wins and losses for any given set of variables that define an edge . If every loss puts you that much closer to a win, you will be looking forward to the next occurrence of your edge, ready and waiting to jump in without the slightest reservation or hesitation. On the other hand, if you still believe that trading is about analysis or about being right, then after a loss you will anticipate the occurrence of your next edge with trepidation, wondering if it's going to work. This, in turn, will cause you to start gathering evidence for or against the trade. You will gather evidence for the trade if your fear of missing out is greater than your fear of losing. And you will gather information against the trade if your fear of losing is greater than your fear of missing out. In either case, you will not be in the most conducive state of mind to produce consistent results. 4. An edge is nothing more than an indication of a higher probability of one thing happening over another. What above means based on my results if I take 100 trades I will roughly get 80wins 20 losers But I have no way of knowing which trade taken according to my system rules is a winner or loser.That's the random factor of any system not just mine. Hi Davit, What I meant by nothing new here is not your system which is very effective but what I was about to post. I meant the indi in its original form is identical to your template(ShowBands=true). It just uses considerably less CPU cycles as I explained in the original post. The yellow is just my choice of the down candle bar color . Go to properties and change the down candle color to Black to remove the yellow. If Show Bands=false it removes the bands and replaces them with extreme levels. What do those "boxes" show?. They are extreme points on the charts defined by your system, that is the rsi is &lt;30 or Greater than 70, and Wpr is &lt;-80 or greater than -20 and the high or the low has crossed the upper BB or the lower BB respectively. The two sets of lines at the top represent the PA crossing of the BBs (solid line Dev=3 &amp; Dotted Dev=2) while RSI and WPR are in extreme overbought zones. These are the high probability resistance levels as defined by your analysis. There is good probability the trend will reverse for a few days which the hooking of the RSI and WPR indicate to you. Vice versa for supports. You will also notice that the extreme levels match previously established levels giving you a high probability of reversal. BB levels tell you that the price, assuming gaussian in nature( Bell Curve) ,will stay 67% of the time between dev= 2 bands and 99% within the dev= 3 bands. So they too indicate a type of extreme level. When a level shows up in the area of the PA , it's an alert that extreme level conditions have been met. If Solid Dev3 and Dev 2. If dotted Dev 2 only. Those who use CCI use the crossing of the + - 100 levels similar to rsi crossing 70 30 from above and below. CCi is just an EMA in a horizontal form. Similarly the 34 EMA that raghee uses, the crossing of the mid line usually coincides with RSI 14 crossing the 50 line. When you mention that the the Rsi is bouncing from the 50 level, the pa would be doing the same with the Raghee Ribbon. Hope this answers some questions raised here and proves beneficial in your trading. Cheers Pat
